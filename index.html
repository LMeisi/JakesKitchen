Red Braised Pork

Red Braised Pork originated from the Chinese province of Hunan, but nowadays it is more associated with Shanghai. It is famous for being a favorite dish of Mao Tse-tung, the founding father of China, and it is often referred to as Chairman Mao's red braised pork.

House Special Okra

Okra first arrived in Japan via the United States during the Meiji period, when Japan opened up to foreign trade in the late 19th century. Our kitchen only imports the highest-grade fresh okra from Japan and upon order, soak them in a house special sauce marinade, bringing you a flavorful classic dish.

Yangchou Pearl Fried Rice

Perhaps the most well-known dish of the city of Yangchou, China. The recipe was invented by Qing China's Yi Bingshou (1754–1815) and the dish was named Yangchou fried rice since Yi was once the regional magistrate of Yangchou. It is often served with thousand fish soup.
